Bansuri

Description :

A Bansuri is a side blown flute originating from the Indian subcontinent. It is an aerophone produced from bamboo, used in Hindustani classical music. A bansuri is traditionally made from a single hollow shaft of bamboo with six or seven finger holes. Some modern designs come in ivory, fiberglass and various metals. The six hole instrument covers two and a half octaves of music.The bansuri is revered as Lord Krishna's divine instrument and is often associated with Krishna's "Rasa Lila Dance".

Other names like Baanhi, Baashi, Bansi, Basari, Murali etc.
