Ghungroo

Description :

A Ghungroo also known as ghunghroo or ghunghru or ghungur or ghungura (Assamese & Bengali & Odia) or Chilanka or Silangai (in Malayalam & Tamil respectively), is one of many small metallic bells strung together to form ghungroos, a musical anklet tied to the feet of classical Indian dancers.A string of ghungroos can range from 50 to greater than 200 bells knotted together.Ghungroos are worn in traditional performances of the classical Indian dance forms: Bharatnatyam, Kathak, Kuchipudi, Mohiniyattam, Lavani, Odissi etc.Ghunghroos are used in folk music, bhajans and abhangs to add layers to the rhythm.
