Indian Musical Instruments

India comprises of some of the most exotic and melodious Indian musical instruments that comprise of the Sitar,Sarod,Sarangi,Flute,Tabla,Shehnai,Harmonium,Dhol, Pakhawaj, Mridamgam, Jaltarangam, Ghatam and many more. These musical instruments are not only famous in India but also all over the world including the USA, the UK and rest of the countries.The musical instruments of India bring along a rich heritage and form an integral part of the cultural traditions of this country. Let’s have an insight of some of the best musical instruments that will make Indians proud with glory.


String Instruments

A frequent trait of many Indian string instruments is the presence of sympathetic strings as well as played strings. The sympathetic strings aren’t often plucked or bowed directly by the musician. Instead they respond to the vibrations coming from the played strings to create their own sound. The body of the instrument also vibrates, along with the air inside it. The vibration of the body of the instrument and the enclosed hollow or chamber make the vibration of the string more audible to the performer and audience. The body of most string instruments is hollow.

Wind Instruments

A wind instrument is a musical instrument that contains some type of resonator (usually a tube) in which a column of air is set into vibration by the player blowing into (or over) a mouthpiece set at or near the end of the resonator. The pitch of the vibration is determined by the length of the tube and by manual modifications of the effective length of the vibrating column of air.Playing some wind instruments, in particular those involving high breath pressure resistance, produce increases in intraocular pressure, which has been linked to glaucoma as a potential health risk. In the case of some wind instruments, sound is produced by blowing through a reed; others require buzzing into a metal mouthpiece, while yet others require the player to blow into a hole at an edge, which splits the air column and creates the sound.

Membranophone Instruments

A membranophone is any musical instrument which produces sound primarily by way of a vibrating stretched membrane.Most membranophones are drums,tabla,dumaroo,nagada,etc.

Idiophone Musical Instruments

An idiophone is any musical instrument that creates sound primarily by the vibration of the instrument itself,without use of air flow,strings,membranes or electricity.Most percussion instrument are not drums are idiophones.Example of Idiophone include the triangle,ghungroo,ghatam,jal-tarang,etc.
